01 Approach

Governance is not a checklist. It is a control system.

Most organisations stack frameworks. ISO 42001 sits next to ISO 27001, which sits next to NIST AI RMF, which sits next to whatever the EU AI Act demands. Each gets its own audit, its own owner, its own spreadsheet.

This is expensive, slow, and produces gaps at the seams — exactly where high-risk AI fails.

I work the other direction. The frameworks are different views of the same problem: what could go wrong, who is accountable, and how do we prove it didn't. When you map the controls across ISO 42001, ISO 31000, ISO 27001, NIST AI RMF, the EU AI Act, and CSA AICM, the overlaps are roughly 70%. The remaining 30% is where you focus — and that's only visible if you treat them as one system.

This is the work. Harmonising controls. Closing seams. Producing a single source of truth a regulator, an auditor, and an engineer can all read.

Six frameworks. One control system. The overlap is the point.

02 NTNU Norway
ERCIM Fellowship
2019 — 2020

Robustness of vision models to colour distortions

ContextImage quality degrades silently in production — bad sensors, lossy pipelines, lighting shifts. Adversarial robustness was well-studied; everyday colour distortions were not.

WorkBuilt one of the earliest datasets designed specifically to study colour-channel distortions on trained deep models. Measured failure modes across architectures.

GovernanceExactly the distribution-shift evidence the EU AI Act requires under robustness testing for high-risk vision systems. Six years before the regulation existed, the experimental playbook was already there.

OutcomeTwo peer-reviewed publications. Best Paper award (Imaging and Multidimensional Signal Processing). Cited in subsequent robustness work.

03 Luleå University
2021 — 2023
Nature Sci. Data

Bimodal EEG + fMRI dataset for inner-speech detection

ContextBrain-computer interfaces for inner-speech rely on either EEG or fMRI alone. No public dataset combined both with synchronised acquisition.

WorkLed the data pipeline end-to-end — acquisition design, preprocessing, alignment, validation. Released through Nature Scientific Data and OpenNeuro under FAIR principles.

GovernanceNeural data is among the most sensitive personal data categories under GDPR. The release process required IRB-equivalent review, anonymisation, and a reusable consent framework.

OutcomePublic dataset on OpenNeuro, cited by downstream BCI research.

04 Luleå × AIDIH
CVPR Workshop
Applied AI North

Nordic Vehicle Dataset — vehicle detection in snow

ContextAutonomous-driving vision benchmarks under-represent winter conditions. Models trained on KITTI or Cityscapes degrade sharply in snow, low light, and occlusion — exactly Nordic operating conditions.

WorkCo-built the Nordic Vehicle Dataset and the accompanying benchmark. Released at a CVPR Workshop.

GovernanceAdverse-weather robustness is exactly what regulators ask for under the EU AI Act's reliability requirements for high-risk vision systems. This work quantifies how off-the-shelf detectors degrade outside their training distribution — the kind of evidence a robustness assessment needs.

OutcomePublic dataset and benchmark. Cited in subsequent adverse-weather perception work.

05 Fraunhofer HHI
ERCIM Fellowship
2023 — 2024

Video quality assessment for next-generation codecs

ContextNext-generation video codecs need quality metrics that match human perception, not just bit-level fidelity. Modern competitions are dominated by 300M-parameter deep learning models that are accurate but uninterpretable.

WorkAt the Video Coding Systems group, benchmarked PSNR, MS-SSIM, and VMAF across emerging codecs. Then competed in the AIM 2024 Compressed VQA Challenge (ECCV Workshops) as a solo entry, with an interpretable Full-Reference method using classical transform-based features and SVR-based multimethod fusion.

GovernanceMeasurement-method choice is itself a governance decision. The wrong metric produces compliant numbers and worse user experience. Interpretability matters in regulated AI — a model whose features you can explain is a model an auditor can defend.

OutcomePlaced 4th of 6 finalists at AIM 2024 ECCV Workshop — the only Full-Reference and only interpretable solution in the finals, against established stalwarts running 90M–390M parameter VLM-based models. ERCIM-funded research at Fraunhofer HHI.

07 About

The thread is signal processing.

I started as an electronics and communications engineer, did a master's in IT in Bangalore, and a PhD in computer science at IIIT Design & Manufacturing Kancheepuram. The thread through all of it has been signal processing — first electrical signals, then images, then video, then the messy multimodal data inside a hospital.

Two ERCIM fellowships took me to Norway and Germany. A research role in Sweden's Arctic north taught me how to build datasets and infrastructure from scratch. I now live in Stockholm and work at the intersection of applied AI research and AI governance — building healthcare AI systems under the EU's TEF-Health programme and harmonising the regulatory frameworks they have to live under.

When I'm not navigating AI regulations, I'm navigating trails. Hiking is how I reset.
